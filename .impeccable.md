## Design Context

### Users
Photographers who use this platform to deliver galleries to clients. Clients are the end-users of the public-facing pages — they've just had a wedding, event, or portrait session and are viewing their delivered photos. They're often browsing on mobile, emotionally connected to the content.

### Brand Personality
Cinematic · Intimate · Timeless

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark theme — photography looks best against dark backgrounds; colors and tones pop, the viewing experience feels cinematic rather than clinical. Warm dark backgrounds (not cold blue-black), cream/off-white text, amber-gold accent. Fonts: Bodoni Moda (display, editorial high-contrast serif) + Jost (body, geometric clean sans). Neither is in the banned-font list.

References: High-end photography studio websites, editorial magazines (Vogue, WSJ Magazine). Anti-reference: Generic SaaS dashboards, admin panels.

### Design Principles
1. **Cinematic first** — every page should feel like a movie still, not a product listing
2. **Space is content** — generous whitespace, resist the urge to fill every pixel
3. **Typography carries weight** — Bodoni Moda display type + Jost body creates a distinct editorial voice
4. **Warm dark** — oklch near-blacks tinted toward 60° (amber), not cold gray or blue-black
5. **Motion is restraint** — one well-placed reveal beats ten micro-interactions
